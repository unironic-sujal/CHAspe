CHAspe: Lunar Surface Analysis Platform

CHAspe is a cutting-edge platform designed for lunar surface analysis and mapping. Utilizing advanced computer vision and artificial intelligence algorithms, our system accurately detects craters, analyzes surface roughness, and identifies optimal landing zones for lunar missions.

Developed by a team of passionate space enthusiasts and AI specialists, CHAspe aims to democratize access to lunar surface data analysis, making it available to both professional astronomers and space exploration enthusiasts.

Mission Statement

To advance lunar exploration by providing precise, reliable, and accessible tools for surface analysis, empowering researchers, space agencies, and enthusiasts to contribute to humanity's understanding of our closest celestial neighbor.

Core Technologies

Computer Vision AI

Our proprietary computer vision algorithms can identify craters with 95% accuracy across various lighting conditions and surface types. The system is trained on thousands of lunar surface images to ensure reliable detection.

Surface Roughness Analysis

CHAspe calculates precise roughness indices by analyzing surface variations, crater density, and terrain features. This metric is crucial for evaluating landing site safety and planning rover traversal routes.

Landing Zone Identification

By combining crater detection with roughness analysis, our system automatically identifies potential landing zones, ranking them based on safety parameters established by space agencies.

Landing Spot Analysis

Our landing spot analysis feature identifies safe landing zones by evaluating multiple factors:

  • Surface flatness and stability
  • Proximity to hazardous features like large craters
  • Terrain accessibility for equipment deployment
  • Solar exposure for power generation
  • Communication line-of-sight with Earth

Each potential landing site receives a comprehensive safety score and is visually marked on the processed image for easy identification.

How CHAspe Works

Step 1: Image Acquisition

Upload or Capture Lunar Surface Images

Users can upload existing lunar surface photographs or connect to telescope feeds for real-time capture. The system accepts images in various formats (JPG, PNG, WEBP) and optimizes them for processing.

Step 2: Pre-processing

Image Enhancement and Normalization

Before analysis, images undergo several pre-processing steps including contrast adjustment, noise reduction, and feature enhancement to improve detection accuracy regardless of original image quality.

Step 3: Crater Detection

AI-Powered Feature Recognition

Our multi-scale detection algorithm identifies craters of various sizes simultaneously, marking their boundaries and calculating key measurements such as diameter, depth estimation, and rim characteristics.

Step 4: Surface Analysis

Roughness Calculation and Terrain Mapping

The system analyzes crater density and distribution to generate a roughness index - a crucial metric for understanding surface characteristics and potential landing hazards.

Step 5: Landing Zone Identification

Safe Landing Site Recommendations

Based on all collected data, CHAspe identifies and highlights optimal landing zones that meet safety requirements for various mission types, from crewed landings to robotic exploration.
